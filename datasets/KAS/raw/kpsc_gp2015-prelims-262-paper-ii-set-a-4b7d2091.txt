The world dismisses curiosity by 
calling it idle or mere idle curiosity even

out with the argument that he has learnt to

to

something about knowledge of the past

questions for sake of the consequences as 
thousands are working now, for example, in 
search of a cure for cancer. But this is not 
the primary function of the scholar, for the 
consequences are usually subordinate to the 
satisfaction of curiosity.
